Why another extension?

The concept being discussed here is based on the book "Atomic Habits" authored by James Clear. In the book, the author presents a rule for breaking a habit which suggests making it difficult to continue the habit. This means adding obstacles or barriers that make it challenging for individuals to engage in the habit. By doing so, people can reduce the likelihood of engaging in the habit and eventually break free from it. This solution is effective because it requires individuals to be intentional and put in effort to overcome the barriers, thus making it harder to fall back into the habit. In summary, the idea is to make it difficult to continue a habit in order to eliminate it entirely.

One effective way to break a habit is to make it harder to continue. By adding obstacles or barriers, individuals can reduce the likelihood of engaging in the habit and eventually eliminate it altogether.
